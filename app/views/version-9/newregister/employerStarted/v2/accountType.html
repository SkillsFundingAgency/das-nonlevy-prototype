Accessing government funding

To pay for your apprenticeships using government funding and to have full access to your apprenticeship account you will need to confirm who you are.

To do this you will need to add your organisation’s PAYE scheme.

You can do this using either of the following:

  • the Government Gateway login for your PAYE scheme
  • your accounts office reference number and employer PAYE scheme reference
What’s an employer PAYE scheme reference?
The employer PAYE scheme reference is in two parts. The first part is 3 digits, which identifies the HMRC office number that deals with the organisation’s PAYE. The second part of the reference (which follows a forward slash) is the tax office's reference for the organisation.
Where do I find my Government Gateway login?
Your payroll or finance department should have your organisation’s Government Gateway information if you don’t already know it. If your organisation has a payroll agent, don’t use the agent’s login details. You will need to set up a Government Gateway account if you do not already have one.
What’s an accounts office reference number?
This is your 13-character accounts office reference number and is used as a payment reference. You will need this number if you contact HRMC to discuss anything relating to your PAYE account. You can find your accounts office reference number on the letter HMRC sent you when you first registered as an employer.
